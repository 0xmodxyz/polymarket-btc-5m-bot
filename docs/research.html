001 / Analysis

The portfolio shape, and where the edge appears to come from.

Wallet activity across 30 days of continuous running, every fill mapped, strategy traced.

StrategyPure spread capture on Polymarket BTC 5m Up/Down
Running period30 days (~3,000 active windows, ~100/day)

Net P/L+$3,720 on $30,000 deployed = +12.4% ROI

This wallet is a pure, automated spread-capture market maker running on Polymarket's BTC 5-minute Up/Down markets around the clock. Every trade is a BUY. There are zero SELLs. The bot buys both sides of nearly every market it touches, locks in a paired cost below $1.00, and collects the guaranteed spread when the market resolves. It is not making directional calls. It does not have a view on whether Bitcoin goes up or down. It simply provides liquidity to both sides of the binary simultaneously and harvests the bid-ask spread embedded in the combined purchase cost.

The numbers confirm this with clinical precision. Of ~33,000 trades across ~3,000 windows, all but a rounding error had both sides purchased — a 99% both-sides participation rate. The median paired cost across the entire run is $0.89, meaning the bot locks in an average guaranteed spread of $0.11 per dollar of shares held. The win rate on resolved BUYs is 50.2%, exactly what you'd expect from a coin-flip market where half the capital is correctly placed by construction. ROI of +12.4% on $30,000 deployed translates to roughly $1.24 per window in spread capture from pure mechanical arbitrage, with zero directional risk.

Key number

99% of all markets had both Up and Down purchased. This is the defining structural fact of the strategy. It is not a directional trader with occasional hedges. It is a market maker with consistent both-sides discipline.

The portfolio shape

The universe is one market type: btc-updown-5m-*. Every market in the data carries that pattern. No ETH, no SOL, no sports, no politics. Within BTC 5-minute markets, the bot is indiscriminate about timing: trades appear whenever the combined midpoint price drops below $0.90, in any window where the condition fires.

Trade size is tightly bounded and near-uniform. The median fill is $0.91, the mean $1.05, with a maximum of $2.10 (when buying a 48¢ side yields 3 shares at $1.44). The top 5% of trades by size carry only ~15% of total capital. This is a deliberate design choice: uniform sizing suppresses variance and ensures no single fill creates a loss large enough to matter.

Where the edge appears to come from

The edge is the spread, and it is mechanically guaranteed. The bot targets markets where the combined cost of buying "Up" and "Down" in the same 5-minute window is below $0.90. When a market resolves, one side pays $1.00 per share and the other pays $0.00. If the bot bought equal shares on both sides at a combined cost of $0.89, it has locked in +$0.11 per share regardless of outcome.

Mechanism: Guaranteed spread = 1 - (Up_price + Down_price). When paired cost is $0.89, the locked-in profit per resolution is $0.11 per unit deployed. The actual win rate of 50.2% is irrelevant to the economics.

What you can copy

The mechanical structure of this strategy is fully transparent and straightforward to replicate. For each new btc-updown-5m-* market that opens:

  1. Buy both "Up" and "Down" simultaneously, targeting a combined cost below $0.90.
  2. Size each side at ~$1 per fill using math.ceil(1.0 / price) to meet CLOB $1 minimum.
  3. Execute Up first (harder to fill), Down second only if Up fills.
  4. Hold to resolution without any SELL activity.
  5. The spread is collected automatically at resolution.

What you probably can't copy for free

The operational infrastructure. Running 2ms price checks, managing CLOB WebSocket subscriptions, placing FAK orders with rollback handling, and automating window detection requires production-grade code with persistent feeds. PolySpread Bot packages all of this into a single python main.py command.

002 / Reverse-engineering report

Every fill mapped, the asymmetric profile traced, the math behind the edge.

P/L methodology: Settlement-accounting. Each BUY trade's P/L = shares x $1.00 if the outcome won, or shares x $0.00 if lost, minus the USDC spent. No SELL trades exist in this book. Spread P/L is computed as (1 - paired_cost) x paired_shares per both-sides market.

The punchline

This wallet is a pure automated spread-capture market maker on Polymarket's BTC 5-minute Up/Down markets. The bot buys both sides of virtually every market it enters, holds to resolution, and collects the guaranteed spread embedded in the combined purchase cost falling below $0.90. There is no SELL engine, no exit management, no directional thesis. The 50.2% win rate on resolved BUYs is not the signal. It is a mechanical consequence of buying both sides: exactly half the capital resolves as a winner, which is precisely what a 50/50 binary market produces when you have bought both outcomes.

The economics are simple and mechanical. Median paired cost of $0.89 means the bot locks in an average $0.11 per unit of paired shares on every resolved both-sides market. Across $30,000 of deployed capital, that structural spread generates the majority of the +$3,720 net P/L.

005 / Replication playbook

How to replicate this strategy — the full implementable spec.

One-sentence summary: An automated market maker that buys both sides of every btc-updown-5m-* market at a combined cost below $0.90, holds to resolution for guaranteed spread capture, and uses Up-first execution to prevent orphaned Down positions.

Bot Design Specification

  1. Market resolution: Resolve the current 5-minute window from Gamma API by slug btc-updown-5m-{cycle_start}. Cycle start = int(time.time() // 300) * 300.
  2. Persistent price feeds: Start CLOB WebSocket + midpoll fallback once at boot. Reuse across windows. Prewarm before first window to save ~5s connection delay.
  3. Entry condition: up_mid + down_mid < 0.90 — at least 10¢ guaranteed profit. Check every 2ms.
  4. Share calculation: math.ceil(1.0 / price). Meets CLOB $1 minimum order size while targeting ~$1 per side.
  5. Execution order: Buy Up first (FAK limit). If Up fills, buy Down (FAK limit). If Up fails, skip pair entirely.
  6. Order type: FAK (fill-or-kill) limit buys only. No market orders — FAK fills at target price or not at all.
  7. Risk controls: 90s deadline protection, $5/side max spend per window, $0.05 hard price floor, 2s cooldown between trades.
  8. Hold to settlement: No SELLs. The platform pays $1 for winning shares at resolution.

Rebuild parameters

Budget: $10 per window. Per-side max: $5. Entry threshold: combined < 0.90. Share calc: ceil(1.0/price). Order type: FAK limit. Execution: Up-first. Deadline: 90s before end. Cooldown: 2s. Feeds: CLOB WS + Binance WS (persistent).
